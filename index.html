Project Proposal Guidelines

Heilmeier's Catechism

A set of questions credited to Heilmeier that anyone proposing a research project or product development effort should be able to answer.

  1. What are you trying to do?
  2. How is it done today, and what are the limits of current practice?
  3. What's new in your approach and why do you think it will be successful?
  4. Who cares? If you're successful, what difference will it make to the world? What are the risks and the offs?
  5. How long will it take and who will do what?
  6. How would you measure success?

Elevator Pitch

In the class, you also need to present your ideas in the form of a two-minute elevator pitch. Each group will get 2 minutes of projector time to show their work. You will NOT get more time (so stick to it!).

  1. your team name (as clever as possible!) and your names
  2. What problem are you attacking?
  3. What technology or technologies are you using? What do you plan to build?
  4. What is your hypothesis or what are your hypotheses?
  5. What would it mean if your hypothesis is confirmed and the project works? What would it mean if it doesn’t?
  6. Why should we get excited about your project? Imagine this is a pitch to funders with a suitcase of money on the line.

Poster Presentation Guidelines

A good poster can stand on its own, but isn’t covered in text. You need to attract people to come talk to you, and to have something that can serve as an aid to explaining your work – and that will continue to explain your work even when you’re not around. Some sample good poster templates will be posted on Canvas.

Project Write-up (30%)

30% of your grade will be based on the paper you produce. You will write a paper in the format of a Ubicomp or CHI conference paper (a full paper and NOT a note). You can write up to 10 pages plus the references.

Submissions should read like reasonable drafts of an actual, submittable research paper, albeit perhaps without an empirical study and attendant results. They should address the questions initially laid out in the project plan and be written well enough to get accepted by the reviewers!

Working Demo or a Promo Video (up to Bonus 5% of your grade)

I strongly believe that in order for your work to have an impact, especially in Ubiquitous Computing you need to show something real and convincingly demonstrate the potential of the technology you are building. So if you create a working demo of the system you are building and/or a promo video showing the promise of the technology you can earn up to 5% bonus points.

Progress Report and Initial Demo.

Midway through the semester we will ask for a progress report and an initial demo in the class. The primary goal of this report is to a) ensure that you're making progress, and b) help you start writing up the final report – the report itself only needs to be of reasonable draft quality. The report should include:

  • A good start to a literature review (expect 20+ references for the final version). Make your life easier by using a citation manager like Zotero or Mendeley.
  • A concise problem statement identifying some problem you want to address, that is reasonable in scope and that can be addressed with a ubiquitous computing application
  • A complete and thorough experiment design
  • A set of concise and falsifiable hypotheses
  • A description of your expected contributions to science, public health, etc.
  • A detailed description of what you have built so far and your initial evaluations of it
  • An internal review board (IRB) human participants protocol.  See the Social and Behavioral Studies Application Form under Initial Approval Requests at http://www.irb.cornell.edu/forms. This application requires you to include a plan on how exactly you intend to evaluate the project
  • An updated project plan that includes what has been completed and a description of any changes to the original plan.

Note that you’ll turn the human subjects applications directly to us, rather than going through the IRB. Exception: if you intend to publish this work, the IRB form needs to be submitted to them. It should still go through the teaching team first for a quick look, and you should plan to submit it earlier because the IRB takes time (often more than a month) to review requests.
